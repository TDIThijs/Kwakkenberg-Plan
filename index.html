Practical rules

Protein first. Aim for 150–160 g protein per day. At a deficit, protein preserves muscle while fat drops. Every meal should have a clear protein source: eggs, chicken, fish, yoghurt, cottage cheese, legumes.
Processed food is mostly ultra-processed fat + refined carbs. These spike insulin, don't satisfy hunger, and are easy to overeat. Replacing even 2 takeaway meals per week with home-cooked alternatives typically removes 1000–1500 kcal without feeling like a diet.
Eat before training, not after (for big meals). A light meal 60–90 min before cycling or swimming gives better energy and reduces post-workout overeating.
Alcohol. If you drink regularly, this is where a large chunk of hidden calories often sits. Beer and wine are ~150–200 kcal per glass with zero satiety. Even cutting back 2–3 drinks per week adds up to ~400–600 kcal saved.
Don't eat less on rest days. Muscle repair happens on rest days and needs protein. Keep intake steady — just avoid large carb-heavy meals in the evenings when activity is low.
Timeline. At a 500 kcal/day deficit plus 3–4 training sessions per week, expect 0.4–0.6 kg per week. To lose 10 kg realistically takes 3–4 months. Don't rush it — faster loss at this stage risks losing muscle mass along with fat, which slows your metabolism and weakens the knee further.
